01

Wear Masks

While mpox transmission via respiratory droplets is less common, wearing a mask in crowded or poorly ventilated areas can help minimize the risk. Masks are more crucial if you're in close contact with someone who may be infected or in high-risk settings like healthcare facilities.

02

Wash your hands

Wash your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. Use alcohol-based hand sanitizers if soap and water are unavailable.

03

Use Nose - Rag

Cover your nose and mouth when sneezing or coughing using a tissue or handkerchief (nose-rag) to avoid spreading or coming into contact with infected droplets.

04

Avoid Contacts

Avoid direct physical contact with individuals who have mpox symptoms, such as rashes, lesions, or scabs. Refrain from sharing personal items like bedding, clothing, or towels with potentially infected individuals.
